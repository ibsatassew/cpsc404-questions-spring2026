## Context (optional)

The Trust game by ncase shows how two Copycats (Tit-for-Tat players who begin by cooperating and then mirror the other’s previous move) can spiral into endless retaliation because of a single mistake. 

To address this, the game introduces Copykitten, a forgiving version of Tit-for-Tat that only retaliates after being cheated twice in a row. The results vary depending on the rate of miscommunication:
* At 0% misunderstanding, Copycat performs best.
* At 1–9%, the forgiving Copykitten wins.
* At 10–49%, the unforgiving Always Cheat dominates.
* At 50%, no strategy wins.

As the simulator explains, a small amount of miscommunication encourages forgiveness, but too much leads to widespread distrust, this creates a conflict with Chapter 5’s advice.
